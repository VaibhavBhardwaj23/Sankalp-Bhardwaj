What is Buisness Blasters?

So, first of all lets talk about what buisness blasters is ? It is a Delhi government initiative, this programme seeks to inculcate a business and entreprenuerial mindset amongst students through a specially designed curriculum. It encourages students to become job creator rather than job-seekers. In 2021-22 , 300,000+ students of grade 11 & 12 of 1000+ government schools of Delhi recieved seed funding of rupees 2000 each for a buisness idea that they developed in a team with the objective of either earning profit or creating social impact . Most of students belong to the most disadvantaged socio-economic communities .

It is world's largest startup programme for students . As a part of Buisness Blasters program , students work in teams , brainstrom , identify buisness opportunities , prepare business plans and implement their buisness ideas in the real world . It is a part of the Entrepreneurship Mindset Curriculum introduces in all Delhi government schools . The selected teams with the most viable ideas and buisness plans progressed to round 2 of the program . These teams were then matched with business coaches and experts from various fields who helped them in taking their businesses to the next level . Finally , panels of ivestors and entrepreneurs hand-picked top 100+ investment-worthy teams that are well on their way towards building profitable and scalable buisness . Details of each round is as follows :

  • Round 1 : Formation of teams and brainstorming on buisness idea . The teams ideated on market opprotunities or social problems they wanted to work on . Guidlines were shared with school teachers to facitilate the process in step-by-step manner .
  • Round 2 : Intense coaching and imroving the plan and the idea . The 1000+ teams which progressed to the second round recieved more intensive coaching from identified Buisness Coaches or mentors to develop their projects or buisness plans . In all , a total of 1000 of buisness coaches worked with the 1000 teams.
  • Round 3 : Showcasing the buisness to the investors - the final round involved showcasing and pitching the projects in an investment summit , to the companies and entrepreneurs , potential investors and philanthropists , repensentatives from incubators , universities , etc . A large stadium was booked for exhibiting the buisnesses and providing an opportunity for the investors to meet and talk to the selected teams . With the live coverage on radio and television channels , the expo not only provided a platform for the teams to explore oppotrtunities for collaborations , investments , and buisness partnerships with the interested visitors in the event , but it also created a lot of enthusiasm among the parents and their children for entrepreneurship.

Market

The crochet and knitting buisness in India is a niche but growing market ,driven by several factors such as increasing interest in handmade products , the popularity of DIY crafts,and the demand for personalised or sustainable fashion. Have a look at the overview of the market dynamics

  1. Growing Interest In Handicrafts-
    • In recent years , there has been a resurgence of interest in traditional and handmade products , which includes crochet and knitting.Many people are turning to buy knitting and crochet products for decors or for themselves too.
    • Social Media platforms like Instagram and Pinterest play a significant role in promoting these crafts , especially with influencers and content creators sharing their DIY creations , tutorials , and projects.
  2. Sustainability and Eco-Friendliness-
    • The increasing awareness sustainability and eco-conscious living has boosted the demand for handmade and natural fiber products. Crochet and knitted products , especially those made from organic cotton, bamboo,or wool, appeal to consumers looking for sustainable alternatives to mass-produced items .
    • This shift towards eco-friendly products is likely to support longterm growth in this market.
  3. Market Segments-
    • Home Decor : Crochet and knitting are popular for making home decor items like throws,cushions and rugs. With peopleinvesting in interior design, these handmade items are gaining popularity.
    • Apparel : Knitwear , sweaters , shawls, and scarves are always in demand. There's growing market for unique,hand-knitted fashion items , especially in urban areas .
    • Baby products : Crochet baby clothes , hats , and blankets are very popular , with many customers preferring them for their softness and uniqueness.
    • DIY and Craft Supplies : There is also a market for knitting and crochet supplies,including yarn , hooks , and patterns. These products have seen a rise in demand with the growing interest in DIY crafts.
  4. Market Trends-
    • Online Sales : E-commerce platforms like Amazon , Flipkart , and niche craft marketplace have made it easier for small buisness owners to sell crochet and knitted products. Social media platforms like Instagram and Facebook provide a direct sales channel for artisans and hobbyists.li>
    • Workshops and Online Learning : The demand for online crochet and knitting classes , workshops , and tutorial has surged, which contributes to the growth of the market . Many platforms are offering free and paid lessons to cater to the increasing interest in learning these crafts .
  5. Challenges -
    • Competition from Mass-Produced Products: Mass-produced knitted or crocheted items from low-cost countries(like China) pose a challenge to local artisans.
    • Lack of Awarenwss and Skill Gaps : While crochet and knitting have potential in the Indian market , there is a gap in knowledge and skill, especially in rural areas. However, the urban market seems more aware and enthusiastic about these crafts .
  6. Economic Potential -
    • The crochet and knitting industry in India has the potential to tap into both local and international markets , with exports of handmade goods being a significant opportunity.
    • Handmade crochet and knitted goods can also appeal to international buyers , particularly in markets like the U.S. and Europe, where there is a demand foor unique and artisanal products.
The crochet and knitting market in India is growing , fueled by combination of changing consumer preferences towards sustainability and handmade products, an increasing online presence , and the rise of DIY culture. There is significant potential for expansion, particularly in niche markets such as personalized products , home decor , and sustainable fashion. However , the market also faces challenges in terms of competition from cheaper mass-produced goods and a gap in widespread awareness and skills.
